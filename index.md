# Learning Web Development the Hard Way

## I’m an online web development student navigating fast-paced courses, burnout, deadlines, and the reality of learning to code without pretending it’s easy.

---

## Why This Site Exists

Most content about learning web development focuses on tools, timelines, and success stories. Very little talks about what happens when the pace is too fast, the assignments pile up, and you’re still trying your best.

This site exists to document the real experience of learning web development in an accelerated online program — the confusion, the frustration, the small wins, and the decision to keep going anyway.

---

## Who This Is For

This site is for:

* Online web development and computer science **students**

* **Career-switchers** learning to code

* **Anyone** overwhelmed by fast-paced tech education

* **Developers** who remember what it felt like to struggle

If you’ve ever thought, ___“I’m trying, but I still can’t keep up,”___ you’re in the right place.

---

## About Me

I’m a web development student learning in an accelerated online program. I write about the parts of learning to code that don’t fit neatly into timelines or course descriptions — falling behind, pushing through burnout, and staying in the program when quitting feels easier.

This site isn’t about shortcuts. It’s about _honesty_.

I am also using this as a way to document my journey and be able to have a way to vent and journal through this process. I am in my 40's and in college online through [Full Sail University](https://www.fullsail.edu/) working my way towards my bachelors degree in Web Development.

Hello, my name is **Angella McConnell**. I am so glad that you have found my page and I truly hope that my writing can possibly help someone going through these same struggles as I am facing. Feel free to reach out if you feel the need or if you are just simply wanting to say hello.
